U.S. Census Bureau and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System - 2014 Findings


Below you will find an interactive chart summarizing 2014 U.S. Census Bureau data by state to discover health risks facing particular demographics.

The X-Axis (independent variables) includes:

  • % In Poverty
  • Median Age
  • Median Household Income

    • The Y-Axis (dependent variables) includes:

  • % Obese
  • % Smokers
  • % Lacks Healthcare


    • Observations:

    Some observations we can see include positive correlations between age & % smokes as well as % in poverty with lacking healthcare. We see negative correlations between lacking healthcare & age, smoking & household income, and obesitiy & household income.

    By state, we see that Southern states tend to have a higher percentage of being in poverty as well as lacking healthcare. Our Counties capital, and the surrounding states have the highest income with the lowest % of lacking healthcare.
